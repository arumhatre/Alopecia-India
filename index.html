Types of alopecia

Androgenic Alopecia

Common in men (Male Pattern Baldness) and women (Female Pattern Baldness),
often due to genetics or hormonal changes

Alopecia Areata

Patchy hair loss caused by an autoimmune disorder

Alopecia Totalis

Complete scalp hair loss, caused due to untreated Alopecia Areata

Alopecia Universalis

Total hair loss on the body, linked to severe Alopecia Areata

Traction Alopecia

Traction Alopecia is hair loss caused by constant pulling, mainly affecting women,
and can become permanent if not treated

FAQs

What is Alopecia Areata?

Alopecia Areata is an autoimmune disease that disrupts your hair growth cycle. Our immune system mistakenly attacks our hair follicles, causing hair loss in patches. It starts with mild hair loss gradually leading to bald spots. People suffering from this condition often have low confidence and self-esteem issues.

What are the types of Alopecia?

Alopecia Areata can occur in various ways, depending on the severity and extent of hair loss. The three main types are Androgenic Alopecia, Alopecia Areata, Alopecia Totalis, and Alopecia Universalis. Each type presents unique challenges and considerations for treatment and management, impacting individuals differently in their daily lives.

What are the causes of Alopecia Areata?

Alopecia Areata is caused because of a malfunction of the immune system and mistakenly targets the hair follicles which lead to hair loss. Other factors like genetics, stress, hormonal changes, and certain medical conditions like eczema or diabetes might be seen alongside Alopecia Areata.

What are the main Alopecia symptoms?

The main signs and symptoms of alopecia are gradual thinning, small bald patches, or complete loss of scalp or body hair. Changes in hair texture, excessive shedding, and scalp irritation may also occur. Since symptoms can vary depending on the type of alopecia and individual factors, it's important to consult a professional healthcare provider.

At what age does Alopecia begin? Can it also be seen in children?

Developing alopecia is not limited to a specific age group. Alopecia Areata often first appears during childhood or adolescence, but it can affect people of all ages. Androgenetic alopecia, which is more common in adults, may start in the late teens or early twenties and progress with age.
